Where was it negotiated?

It was negotiated at Lower Fort Garry aka Stone Fort
located in present day Manitoba, Canada. The negotiations
began on July 27th and concluded August 3rd in 1871.
Apprximately 1000 Indigenous people including men, women and
and children attended the negotiations, forming a large
camp around the fort.

Why are treaties important?

Treaties are considered the "supreme Law of the Land" and they are
important today because they are legally binding agreements that recognize
independence, protect rights and guide governance between Nations and Indigenous
peoples. For Indigenous Nations, treaties affirm their in-born sovereignty, allowing
them to govern their lands, manage resources and maintain nation-to-nation
relationships with the federal government.
